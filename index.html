Introduction to "King Kohli"

Virat Kohli, born November 5, 1988, is an Indian international cricketer and former captain of the Indian national cricket team. Considered one of the greatest batsmen of all time, Kohli plays as a right-handed batsman for Delhi in domestic cricket and Royal Challengers Bangalore in the IPL. He is often referred to as "King Kohli" due to his dominant performances across all formats of the game.

Kohli made his international debut in 2008 and quickly established himself as a prolific run-scorer. His aggressive batting style and impeccable consistency have led him to break numerous records and guide India to many historic victories.

Key Achievements & Milestones

Most Centuries in ODIs

Surpassed Sachin Tendulkar's record for most One Day International centuries.

A testament to his incredible longevity and consistency in the 50-over format.

Leading Run-Scorer in T20Is

Holds the record for the most runs in Twenty20 International cricket.

Known for his exemplary chase master abilities and striking big in crucial T20 games.

ICC Player of the Decade (2010s)

Awarded the Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for male cricketer of the decade.

Recognized for his unparalleled dominance and influence on world cricket throughout the 2010s.

Captaincy Records

Most Test wins as Indian captain globally and highly successful in all formats.

Under his leadership, India reached new heights, especially in Test cricket.

Notable Records & Statistics

  • Most ODI centuries: 50+ centuries and counting.
  • Fastest to 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, 11,000, 12,000, and 13,000 ODI runs.
  • Only player to score 20,000 international runs across all formats in a decade (2010s).
  • Most runs in a calendar year in ODIs by an Indian: 1,377 runs in 2018.
  • First player to score centuries against all 9 Test-playing nations.
  • Most Player of the Series awards in T20Is.
  • Highest career batting average in T20I history (for players with significant matches).
  • Only captain to score centuries in a World Cup and a Champions Trophy final (though not final wins).
